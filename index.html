Forensic Methodologies, Citizen Science, and Governmental Investigations in UAP Research

An Integrated Project Roadmap to Professionalize Forensic Investigation in UAP Studies

Project Overview

The Society for UAP Studies (SUAPS) is leading an innovative interdisciplinary project to address critical gaps in the field of forensic methodologies within both citizen-led and governmental UAP investigations. This SUAPS-led initiative explores forensic investigative methodologies within citizen science and governmental entities involved in UAP research. It aims to establish conceptual clarity, rigorous methodological standards, and structured best practices to guide and professionalize future UAP investigations, thus significantly contributing to the scholarly legitimacy of UAP Studies.

Core Objectives

By fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and establishing robust investigative guidelines, this initiative aims to significantly enhance the rigor, consistency, and academic acceptance of forensic UAP studies.

  • Establish a standing interdisciplinary working group addressing forensic methodologies, citizen science, and governmental UAP investigations.
  • Define and formalize best practices, methodologies, and standards for forensic UAP investigations.
  • Investigate the integration of citizen-science practices and governmental forensic methods, examining interactions, challenges, and opportunities.
  • Facilitate scholarly dialogue between data science, forensic science, citizen scientists, and governmental investigators.
  • Disseminate findings through scholarly publications, symposia, workshops, and the publication of a major white paper.

Key Components: Working Group & Events

Working Group Structure

Mission: To conceptualize, analyze, and formalize best practices in forensic methodologies for citizen and governmental UAP investigations.

Membership: The group will be comprised of researchers from forensic sciences, data science and information systems, UAP/UFO empirical research, philosophy of science, and citizen science experts.

Governance: The group will be chaired by Drs. Eric Halford and Joshua Pierson, supported by expert scholars and professional advisors, including data and information science specialists.

Academic & Professional Events

A series of scholarly events will be organized to collaboratively refine methodological approaches and share insights, including an initial workshop and a subsequent public symposium. Potential partners include Stanford University's SOL project and Erlangen University's Center for Alternative Rationalities.

Project Timeline & Milestones

Phase 1: Planning & Preparation (Immediate - May 2025)

Confirm workshop locations, institutional partnerships, and event agendas. Finalize participant profiles and thematic focus areas.

Phase 2: Participant Recruitment (June - July 2025)

Extend invitations and confirm commitments from scholars, institutional partners, and expert practitioners.

Phase 3: Pre-Event Preparations (August - September 2025)

Gather preliminary abstracts and proposals. Prepare logistics and promotional materials.

Phase 4: Workshop Execution (October - November 2025)

Execute the initial workshop, focusing on paper development, feedback, and methodology standardization.

Phase 5: Scholarly Outputs & White Paper (November 2025 - March 2026)

Publish workshop proceedings in a special issue of Limina: The Journal of UAP Studies and develop a comprehensive white paper on forensic methodologies.

Phase 6: Symposium & Dissemination (Spring 2026)

Organize a public symposium based on workshop findings to foster interdisciplinary dialogue and expand academic visibility.
